What is wrong with the Current Lending Industry?

x

The current credit market hasn’t evolved since the 1800’s. Loans are accepted, rejected and priced mostly by people. Almost like insurance, when someone applies for loans from banks or credit cards, there are a handful of metrics used to gauge the risk of the consumer. As an example, if your FICO score is under 650, most banks will reject your application, regardless of the cause of the low FICO score or your assets. The lack of personalization implies there should be significant inefficiencies in the market. It also implies that customers are grouped together, likely resulting in several subgroups of customers overpaying and subsidizing several other subgroups.
